# Biases
**정보 신선도:** 🟢  |  **최종 갱신:** 2026-04  |  **다음 갱신:** 2026-07

## Purpose
This file lists the explicit forecast biases that should be corrected during drafting.

## Known biases to correct
- Commercialization timing is often assumed too early.
- Chinese technical progress is often understated, especially when judged through Western chokepoint narratives alone.
- Energy, water, cooling, grid, and materials constraints are often treated as secondary.
- US hegemony is often assumed to persist without institutional or fiscal degradation.
- Regulatory and standards friction is often priced in too lightly.
- Model capability gains are often mistaken for deployment gains.
- Corporate roadmaps are often repeated without discounting for yield, cost, or go-to-market drag.

## Correction rules
- For each optimistic milestone, write one explicit delay path.
- For each hardware or infrastructure claim, identify at least one physical bottleneck.
- For each geopolitical claim, identify at least one domestic political constraint.
- For each climate claim, separate scientific signal from policy response.
- For each labor claim, distinguish automation capability from adoption economics.

## Drafting checklist
- Is this statement sourced or inferred?
- If inferred, is the inference labeled?
- Have official claims been discounted based on prior execution history?
- Are institutional and physical constraints listed separately?
- Does the file include Base, Upside, and Downside?
