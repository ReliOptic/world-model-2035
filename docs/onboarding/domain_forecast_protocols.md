# Domain-Specific Forecast Protocols

**정보 신선도:** 🟢  |  **최종 갱신:** 2026-05  |  **다음 갱신:** 2026-08  
**Issue scope:** #31, #35. This file reduces domain bias in the general guide.

## Common protocol
All sectors should separate:

1. current state
2. official roadmap
3. repo forecast
4. Base / Upside / Downside
5. bottlenecks
6. prediction-log candidates
7. source freshness

## Technology and infrastructure
Best fit for FLP-style constrained-path reasoning.

- Prioritize physical capacity, capex, supply chain, standards, adoption rate, and lead time.
- Ask whether demand is ahead of grid, compute, cooling, fabrication, or construction capacity.
- Translate technical milestones into deployment constraints.

## Geopolitics
Do not force geopolitics into a pure capex model.

- Prioritize policy intent, alliance structure, military posture, sanctions, domestic legitimacy, and escalation risk.
- Use scenario bands for policy discontinuities.
- Treat official statements as strategic communication, not neutral roadmaps.

## Labor and economics
Focus on institutional transmission rather than technology capability alone.

- Track wages, productivity, bargaining power, labor-market slack, fiscal support, interest rates, and diffusion of automation.
- Separate task automation from job displacement.
- Include distributional effects when possible.

## Biotech and health
Use regulatory and clinical gates as primary constraints.

- Track trial phase, safety data, approval pathway, reimbursement, manufacturing, and public acceptance.
- Avoid assuming research success equals adoption.
- Use explicit timelines for clinical evidence and policy approval.

## Climate, science, and international organizations
Treat uncertainty and governance capacity as first-order variables.

- Track observation quality, model uncertainty, tipping-risk confidence, institutional mandate, funding, and compliance power.
- Separate scientific finding, policy recommendation, and enforceable action.
- Use confidence ranges where binary prediction would be misleading.
