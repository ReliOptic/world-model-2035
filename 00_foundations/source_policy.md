# Source Policy
**정보 신선도:** 🟢  |  **최종 갱신:** 2026-04  |  **다음 갱신:** 2026-07

## Purpose
Define which sources are acceptable for substantive repository claims and how to record them.

## Primary source rule
- Prefer official, institutional, regulatory, or peer-reviewed sources.
- Treat company blog posts and press releases as official claims, not neutral facts.
- Treat secondary reporting as a pointer, not a terminal source, whenever a primary source exists.

## Domain source hierarchy
- Climate and nature: WMO, IPCC, national meteorological agencies, peer-reviewed journals
- National strategy and policy: government ministries, legislatures, regulators, OECD and multilateral institutions
- Corporate roadmaps: investor relations materials, earnings calls, technical talks, official product docs
- Economics and finance: IMF, OECD, BIS, central banks, treasury departments, statistical agencies
- Science and medicine: peer-reviewed journals, lab publications, university releases, trial registries where relevant

## Recording rule
Every substantive file should end with:

```markdown
## 정보 출처
- [기관 또는 출처명] [URL] [YYYY-MM-DD]
```

## Writing rule
- Label sourced facts as facts.
- Label reasoned extrapolations as inferences.
- Label unresolved claims as open questions.

## Avoid
- unsourced benchmark claims
- stale roadmap dates without refresh
- vague aggregate claims with no institution or dataset behind them
- copying consensus narratives without checking the underlying primary document
