# Technical Realism Framework
**정보 신선도:** 🟢  |  **최종 갱신:** 2026-04  |  **다음 갱신:** 2026-07

## Goal
Balance frontier-roadmap optimism against deployment reality.

## Optimist lens
- Frontier labs and hyperscalers may continue compressing inference cost and raising capability faster than consensus expects.
- Scientific AI systems may move from narrow prediction into cross-domain law discovery and simulation.
- Robotics, launch, and compute infrastructure may scale through sharp nonlinear improvements instead of steady lines.

## Realist lens
- Diffusion takes time even when the core invention is real.
- Adoption depends on standards, capex cycles, labor arrangements, and regulation.
- Bottlenecks in chips, grid power, water, cooling, fabs, rare materials, and geopolitics can dominate software progress.
- Public claims from firms should be discounted by execution history and market incentives.

## Required balancing questions
- What is physically possible by 2035?
- What is economically deployable by 2035?
- What is politically and institutionally permitted by 2035?
- What is likely to diffuse beyond frontier actors by 2035?

## Output rule
Every major topic file should present:
- current verified state
- official roadmap
- discounted interpretation
- annual base case
- upside case
- downside case

## Typical failure modes
- treating laboratory success as mass-market readiness
- confusing benchmark progress with workflow replacement
- assuming policy will respond at technology speed
- assuming the same bottleneck matters across all countries
